## Maze Navigation Strategies

### Primary Strategy: Right-Hand Rule with Compass Directions

Since the camera is fixed (top-down overhead), you navigate using absolute compass directions: north, south, east, west.

**Tracking your "facing direction":**
You don't physically turn — instead, track the direction of your **last move** to determine the right-hand rule priority:

| Last moved | Right | Front | Left | Back (U-turn) |
|-----------|-------|-------|------|----------------|
| North | East | North | West | South |
| East | South | East | North | West |
| South | West | South | East | North |
| West | North | West | South | East |

**Right-Hand Rule Logic (using sensor data):**
1. Call `getPlayerStatus()` to get distances in all 4 directions.
2. Determine your "right/front/left/back" based on your last move direction (see table above).
3. Try directions in priority order: **Right → Front → Left → Back**
4. A direction is "open" if its distance > 2.0m. A direction is "blocked" if distance < 1.5m.

**Example:** If you last moved North, try East first (right). If East is blocked, try North (front). If North is blocked, try West (left). If all blocked, go South (back = U-turn).

### Path Planning with movePath()

**Trace the full visible corridor** from your current position through ALL visible turns, then execute the entire path in one `movePath()` call.

**movePath uses {dir, steps} objects** where `steps` is the number of **grid cells** (count green lines on the floor):
- `movePath([{dir: "north", steps: 3}])` = move north 3 cells
- `movePath([{dir: "east", steps: 2}, {dir: "south", steps: 3}])` = east 2 cells then south 3 cells
- Use `getPlayerStatus()` distances (in cells) to determine how far you can go in each direction
- **Count the green grid lines** in the screenshot to visually verify distances

**⚠️ MANDATORY: Always plan MULTIPLE segments.**
Do NOT stop at the first corner. Trace the corridor through ALL visible corners/turns.

**How to plan a path (STEP BY STEP):**
1. Take a screenshot and study the visible corridor layout.
2. Starting from your current position, **trace the entire visible path**:
   - Follow corridor north until wall → count cells → note "north X cells"
   - See opening to east → count cells east until wall → note "east Y cells" 
   - See opening to south → count cells south until wall → note "south Z cells"
   - KEEP GOING until you reach the edge of visibility or a junction with 2+ open choices
3. Write out the full trace in reasoning: "可见路径：北4格→东2格→南3格"
4. Convert to movePath: `movePath([{dir:"north",steps:4},{dir:"east",steps:2},{dir:"south",steps:3}])`

**BAD example (single segment, wastes a screenshot cycle):**
```
movePath([{dir: "north", steps: 4}])   // You can see the east turn at the top! Why stop?
```

**GOOD example (traces full visible path):**
```
movePath([{dir: "north", steps: 4}, {dir: "east", steps: 2}, {dir: "south", steps: 3}])
```

**Rules for path planning:**
- Maximum 20 segments per `movePath()` call, **minimum 2 segments** (except at junctions or dead ends)
- Each segment's `steps` must be an integer between 1 and 10 (grid cells)
- The player snaps to cell center after each segment — no accumulation of floating-point drift
- Plan ONLY within the area you can clearly see in the screenshot
- If unsure about what's ahead (at the edge of visible area), stop the path there and take a new screenshot
- If `blocked` comes back true, the path was interrupted — take a new screenshot and re-plan
- **Dead-end prohibition still applies**: Never include a direction that leads into a visible dead end

**🚫 Visual Override (MANDATORY — overrides the right-hand rule):**
Before executing ANY move, you MUST check the screenshot first. If the corridor you're about to enter has a **visible dead end** (back wall visible, no side openings), you are **FORBIDDEN** from entering it. Skip it and try the next direction in priority. This is not optional — it is a hard rule.

In your reasoning output, you MUST explicitly state for each direction you consider:
- "北方：截屏可见尽头→死胡同，跳过" or "北方：截屏显示通路延伸→可行"

### Dead-End Detection

#### From sensor data:
You've hit a dead end when 3 out of 4 directions have distance < 1.5m.

**Action**: Move in the one open direction to backtrack.

#### From visual observation (proactive — HIGHEST PRIORITY):

🚫 **This check MUST happen BEFORE every move. It is the most important rule.**

**ADVANTAGE: The maze has LOW WALLS.** From the overhead camera you can see the maze layout clearly for many cells around you. Use this to plan ahead — you can often trace a corridor's entire path visually before entering it.

In the screenshot, a dead end looks like:
- A corridor where the **back wall is clearly visible** at the far end
- **No openings** on the left or right sides of that corridor
- The corridor is only 1-3 cells deep with no branches
- Even a long corridor is a dead end if you can see the end wall and there are no side exits
- **With low walls, you can see over them** — look for paths that connect to open areas vs. paths boxed in on all sides

**Action**: ABSOLUTELY DO NOT ENTER. Choose a different direction.

**You MUST state your visual analysis for the direction you're about to move:**
- Example: "截屏分析：东侧通道可见约2格后是墙壁，无左右分支→死胡同，跳过"
- Example: "截屏分析：北方通道较深，可见左侧有开口→可进入"

### Loop Avoidance

If you suspect you're going in circles:
- Track your position coordinates from `getPlayerStatus()` across steps.
- If your position is within ~1 cell of a previously visited position, you may be looping.
- Break the loop by temporarily switching to the **Left-Hand Rule** (swap the priority: Left → Front → Right → Back).

### Goal Awareness (DO NOT override navigation algorithm)

- The goal is a **tall RED pillar with a red glow ring on the ground** — highly visible from a distance.
- **Seeing the RED marker is only informational.** It tells you the goal exists in that direction, but the maze path may require going the OPPOSITE way first to eventually reach it.
- **NEVER abandon the right-hand wall-following algorithm** just because you can see the red marker in a certain direction.
- If `reachedGoal` is `true` in any response from `movePath()` or `getPlayerStatus()`, stop immediately.

### Distance Interpretation

The obstacle distances from `getPlayerStatus()` (in each of the 4 compass directions, measured in **grid cells**):
- `< 0.7 cells`: Wall is immediately blocking — cannot pass in this direction
- `0.7 ~ 1.5 cells`: Wall is one grid cell away — borderline, check screenshot
- `1.5 ~ 2.5 cells`: At least one open cell — safe to move in this direction
- `> 2.5 cells`: Long corridor or large open area — good path to explore

Use the distance values (in cells) to set `steps` in `movePath()`. For example, if eastDistance is 3.6, you can safely do `{dir: "east", steps: 3}` to cover most of the open corridor. **Always round down** to avoid hitting a wall.

### Screen-to-Compass Mapping (Top-Down View)

The camera is directly overhead looking straight down:
- **Up on screen** = North (+Z)
- **Down on screen** = South (-Z)
- **Right on screen** = East (+X)
- **Left on screen** = West (-X)

This is a natural map orientation — north is up, east is right.
